WHY WE NEED
OPEN SOURCE PHARMA

We asked scientists, entrepreneurs, and activists why the time was right for open source pharmaceuticals. Click on the authors' names below to read what they had to say.

BY JAMES ARINAITWE

GLOBAL HEALTH CORPS & ASPEN INSTITUTE FELLOW / KAMPALA

"The beauty of Open Source Pharma is that, first, it brings important ideas and data together in a forum not controlled by patent laws"

BY ELS TORREELE

DIRECTOR OF ACCESS TO ESSENTIAL MEDICINES INITIATIVE, OPEN SOCIETY FOUNDATIONS / NYC

"Instead of viewing medicines as proprietary luxury goods that are owned by private companies, medicines could be public or social goods"

BY JOHN WILBANKS

CHIEF COMMONS OFFICER OF SAGE BIONETWORKS / WASHINGTON, D.C.

"The pharmaceutical industry is broken. Every year, it spends more money to discover fewer drugs than before, and the drugs that do make it to market are priced at levels that exclude all but the very wealthy from access."

BY MANICA BALESEGARAM

EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR, ACCESS CAMPAIGN, MÉDECINS SANS FRONTIÈRES / GENEVA

"We need to fundamentally let go of thinking that there is only one possible business model. We need alternatives"

BY DIMITRIOS TZALIS

FOUNDER & CEO OF TAROS CHEMICALS / DORTMUND

"The pharmaceutical industry has consistently met the medical needs of developed societies, but played a detrimental role in tackling major diseases"

BY TOMASZ SABLINSKI

CEO OF TRANSPARENCY LIFE SCIENCES / NYC

"Fantastic progress in science has not led to enough new medications"

BY MATTHEW TODD

FOUNDER OF OPEN SOURCE MALARIA / SYDNEY & CAMBRIDGE

"Something could be found in radical openness"

BY ZAKIR THOMAS

OPEN INNOVATION EXPERT & FORMER PROJECT DIRECTOR OF OSDD / DEHLI

"The funding pattern of innovation for neglected diseases points to the need for alternative models in drug development"

BY POLLY J. PRICE

EMORY UNIVERSITY LAW PROFESSOR / ATLANTA

"We must find a better way to combat the rising, world-wide threat from drug-resistant tuberculosis"

BY T.V. MOHANDIS PAI

CHAIRMAN OF MANIPAL GLOBAL EDUCATION SERVICES / BANGALORE

"It is essential that we recreate the compassion of the past and the spirit that put human lives first, before commercial interests, and create an ecosystem where individuals and groups can come together in an open system, to create new drugs to eliminate diseases and save lives"

"Why we need Open Source Pharma -- voices from Atlanta to Delhi speak up"

By Open Source Pharma on THUNDERCLAP
